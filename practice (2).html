My approach

Your expertise on your own life

I'm trained in motivational interviewing and narrative therapy, and I mean really trained, having learned directly from the original theorists of both. I gravitate toward these approaches because they fit who I am. They center authenticity, collaboration, and your expertise on your own life.

There's no hierarchy here, and I'm not the expert on you. I show up as a real person: casual, direct, and easy to talk to. Our conversations will feel like conversations.

This isn't case management or reviewing your week or spinning in circles. It's purposeful movement toward a life that feels more like yours.

  • 01

    Clarifying what you actually value, not what you've been told you should

  • 02

    Finding your own reasons to act on what you already know

  • 03

    Unpacking the messages society has fed you about who you should be and what you should have accomplished by now

  • 04

    Assuming you know yourself, and helping you trust that

  • 05

    Deciding together when and how to move on your goals, at a pace that's yours

Good fit

This might be a good fit if you...

You're a good fit if you

  • Have a goal you want to move toward, even if it's still taking shape
  • Feel stuck between intention and follow-through
  • Want a therapist who understands that what's happening in the world right now is genuinely hard, and who won't ask you to bracket that at the door
  • Are looking for a space that is explicitly affirming, politically aware, and grounded in an understanding of the systems shaping your life

.

.

I'm not the right fit for

  • Crisis support
  • People whose needs require more intensive or specialized care

I'll always be honest with you about that, and I'm happy to help you find the right support if that's the case.

About me

A real person with a full life

I've been doing social work in one form or another for about 20 years. My path has been anything but linear. I've worked in child welfare, psychiatric mental health, the VA, and now private practice. I spent over a decade as a social work professor, worked as an ethics researcher at a major tech company, and have consulted and trained across industries.

I've worn a lot of hats, and honestly, that's what makes me good at this.

Outside of work, I'm a caregiver, a devoted dog mom, and someone who will always say yes to a new city, a craft show, an art museum, or a really good cup of coffee. I bring that full life into the room.

I work best with people who want a therapist who's warm, direct, and easy to talk to: someone who takes the work seriously without taking themselves too seriously.
